medium |ˈmiːdɪəm| noun
1 an agency or means of doing something:

2 the intervening substance through which sensory impressions are
  conveyed or physical forces are transmitted:

3 a particular form of storage material for computer files, such as
  magnetic tape or discs.

4 the material or form used by an artist, composer, or writer:

5 a person claiming to be in contact with the spirits of the dead and
  to communicate between the dead and the living.

6 the middle quality or state between two extremes; a reasonable
  balance:

adjective
halfway between two extremes of size, amount, length, etc.; average:
